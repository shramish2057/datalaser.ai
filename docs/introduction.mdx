## How DataLaser is different

Most BI tools either require heavy setup (Tableau, Power BI) or send your raw data to an LLM and hope for the best (every "AI analytics" startup).

DataLaser does neither.

### The verified architecture

DataLaser uses a **templates-first** approach:

1. **Templates compute facts.** Industry-specific templates run statistical analyses locally: correlations, distributions, anomalies, trends, segments, and more.
2. **AI explains facts.** Claude receives only the computed results (aggregates, statistics, patterns), never individual data rows, and generates clear, contextualized explanations.

This means every insight is **mathematically grounded** before AI touches it. No hallucinated numbers. No made-up trends. The AI's job is to explain verified facts in plain language, not to guess at your data.

<Info>
  **Why does this matter?** When an LLM receives raw data, it can fabricate patterns, miscount rows, or invent correlations. DataLaser's architecture makes this structurally impossible. The numbers are always computed by deterministic code first.
</Info>

## Who is DataLaser for?

DataLaser is purpose-built for **German Mittelstand companies and global SMBs**, typically 20 to 500 employees, who:

- Have valuable data in spreadsheets, databases, or SaaS tools
- Don't have (or don't want to hire) a dedicated data engineering team
- Need insights faster than a quarterly consulting engagement
- Care about data privacy and where their information is processed

<Tip>
  DataLaser works with your existing data wherever it lives. You don't need to migrate to a data warehouse or restructure anything before getting started.
</Tip>

## Bilingual intelligence

DataLaser automatically detects whether your data is in **German or English**: column headers, categorical values, date formats, currency symbols, and more. Insights, labels, and explanations adapt accordingly.

No language settings to configure. No manual mapping. Upload a file with `Umsatz`, `Kundenname`, and `Bestelldatum` columns, and DataLaser understands them just as well as `Revenue`, `Customer Name`, and `Order Date`.
