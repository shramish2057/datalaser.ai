## What happens after you connect data

The moment your data source is connected and profiled, DataLaser's analysis engine starts working. Here's the sequence:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Schema analysis">
    DataLaser examines your column names, data types, and value distributions to understand what kind of data you have: sales transactions, customer records, financial bookings, inventory logs, or something else entirely.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Template matching">
    Your schema is matched against **42 industry templates**. Each template encodes domain knowledge about what analyses are meaningful for specific data shapes. A table with `order_date`, `revenue`, and `product_category` triggers e-commerce templates. A table with `Buchungsdatum`, `Betrag`, and `Kostenstelle` triggers German financial templates.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Auto-analysis execution">
    Every matched template runs its applicable analyses. DataLaser executes up to **17 distinct analysis types** depending on your data.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Insight generation">
    Computed statistical results are passed to Claude, which generates plain-language explanations. Remember: Claude sees only aggregated facts, never raw data rows.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  **Typical timing:** For a dataset with 100,000 rows and 20 columns, the full pipeline (profiling, template matching, analysis, and insight generation) completes in under 60 seconds.
</Info>

## The 17 auto-analyses

DataLaser runs the following analyses automatically when your data supports them:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Statistical">
    | Analysis | What it finds |
    |----------|---------------|
    | **Distributions** | How values are spread: normal, skewed, bimodal, long-tail |
    | **Correlations** | Relationships between numeric columns (Pearson, Spearman) |
    | **Descriptive statistics** | Central tendency, spread, quartiles for every numeric column |
    | **Outlier detection** | Values that fall outside expected ranges (IQR and z-score methods) |
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Patterns">
    | Analysis | What it finds |
    |----------|---------------|
    | **Trends** | Directional movement over time, including growth, decline, and seasonality |
    | **Anomalies** | Unexpected spikes, drops, or structural breaks in time series |
    | **Seasonality** | Recurring patterns at weekly, monthly, quarterly, and annual cycles |
    | **Changepoints** | Moments where the underlying pattern shifts significantly |
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Segments">
    | Analysis | What it finds |
    |----------|---------------|
    | **Clusters** | Natural groupings in your data (k-means, DBSCAN) |
    | **Segments** | High-value vs. low-value groups, behavioral cohorts |
    | **Category comparison** | Performance differences across categories |
    | **Pareto analysis** | Which 20% of items drive 80% of results |
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Advanced">
    | Analysis | What it finds |
    |----------|---------------|
    | **Regression** | Key drivers and their relative importance |
    | **Frequency analysis** | Event occurrence patterns and intervals |
    | **Gap analysis** | Missing expected values or time periods |
    | **Cross-tabulation** | Interactions between categorical variables |
    | **Cohort analysis** | How groups behave over time from a shared starting point |
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Note>
  Not every analysis runs on every dataset. DataLaser only executes analyses that are statistically valid for your specific data shape. A dataset without a time column won't trigger trend or seasonality analyses.
</Note>

## Understanding the Insights page

After auto-analysis completes, open the **Insights** tab in your project. Here's what you'll see:

### Insight cards

Each finding is presented as a card containing:

- **Headline**: A one-sentence summary of the finding (e.g., *"Revenue correlates strongly with marketing spend (r=0.87)"* or *"3 distinct customer segments identified"*)
- **Visualization**: A chart, distribution plot, or table that supports the finding
- **Confidence indicator**: Statistical significance and sample size context
- **Methodology note**: Which analysis produced this insight and the parameters used
- **Actions**: Explore in Studio, ask a follow-up, or dismiss

### Insight categories

Insights are grouped by type so you can focus on what matters:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Opportunities" icon="arrow-trend-up">
    Growth patterns, underexploited segments, and positive correlations worth investigating.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Risks" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    Anomalies, declining trends, and outliers that may need attention.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Facts" icon="chart-bar">
    Descriptive findings: distributions, key metrics, and baseline statistics about your data.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Prioritization

DataLaser ranks insights by a combination of:

1. **Statistical strength**: Effect size and significance
2. **Business relevance**: Template knowledge about what matters in your industry
3. **Actionability**: Whether the finding suggests a concrete next step

The most important findings appear first. Scroll down for supporting details and secondary patterns.

## Exploring further in Studio

Any insight can be opened in **Studio** for deeper analysis. Click **"Explore in Studio"** on an insight card to open a notebook pre-loaded with:

- The underlying analysis and its parameters
- The source data query
- Interactive controls to adjust thresholds, time ranges, or groupings

```python
# Example: a Studio cell generated from an anomaly insight
from datalaser import analysis

results = analysis.anomaly_detection(
    source="sales_2026",
    column="daily_revenue",
    method="isolation_forest",
    sensitivity=0.05
)

results.plot(highlight_anomalies=True)
```

In Studio, you can:

- **Adjust parameters**: Change sensitivity thresholds, time windows, or segmentation criteria
- **Chain analyses**: Feed the output of one analysis into another
- **Add visualizations**: Create custom charts beyond the auto-generated ones
- **Export results**: Download as PDF, PNG, or CSV for reports and presentations

<Tip>
  **Studio is optional.** Many users get everything they need from the Insights page alone. Studio is there when you want to go deeper or customize.
</Tip>

## Asking follow-up questions with Ask Data

See an interesting insight and want to drill down? Switch to the **Ask Data** tab and ask a follow-up question in natural language.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Example follow-ups for a correlation insight">
    - *"Is the correlation between marketing spend and revenue consistent across regions?"*
    - *"What's the lag between spend increase and revenue impact?"*
    - *"Gibt es saisonale Unterschiede in der Korrelation?"*
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Example follow-ups for an anomaly insight">
    - *"What else happened on the days with revenue anomalies?"*
    - *"Are the anomalies concentrated in a specific product category?"*
    - *"Show me the 10 transactions from the anomaly on March 15"*
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Example follow-ups for a segmentation insight">
    - *"What distinguishes segment A from segment B?"*
    - *"How has the size of each segment changed over time?"*
    - *"Welches Segment hat die h&ouml;chste Abwanderungsrate?"*
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

Ask Data uses the same verified architecture. Your question is translated into an analysis, computed deterministically, and the results are explained by AI. No raw data is sent to Claude.
